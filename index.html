Priority 2 — New Creative Format Testing

Beyond continuing Dr Felice whitelisting content, we are expanding into 4 new video formats to find what resonates with audiences who may not respond to a talking-head expert format. Here is what each looks like in practice:

POV (Point of View)
Filmed from the viewer's own perspective — as if they are doing the routine themselves. No talking head, no face. Just hands, products, and a mirror. Creates immersion and high relatability for people who want to see what the routine actually looks like day-to-day.
e.g. "You wake up, your skin feels tight. Here's what your new morning looks like."
Talking Head (Non-Expert)
A real customer or creator speaking directly to camera — not a doctor or professional. Casual, unscripted feel. Confessional tone. Works well for skeptic conversion and relatability because it is peer-to-peer, not top-down.
e.g. "I tried everything. I almost gave up. Then someone in my group chat mentioned Moonbow..."
UGC (User-Generated Content Style)
Filmed and edited to look like organic social media content — slightly imperfect lighting, casual captions, natural speech. Designed to blend into the feed so it does not feel like an ad at first. High trust signal because it mimics what a friend would post.
e.g. screen record of someone saving the product, or a phone filming in a bathroom mirror with natural lighting.
Emotional Slideshow
A sequence of still images with on-screen text and music — no video required. Tells a story in 15–30 seconds through before/afters, quotes, and results. Low production cost, easy to iterate quickly. Works especially well for before/after and testimonial angles.
e.g. 5-frame story: "My skin was reacting to everything → I tried 8 products → Nothing worked → Then I found this → 3 weeks later..."
Priority 3 — New Audience Angle Testing
Strategic Context
Our current messaging is anchored on the "reactive skin" angle. While it has driven our best results, it limits our addressable audience to people who already identify with that problem. Testing new angles below allows us to reach people who have the same underlying need — skin that is responding poorly to their lifestyle — but describe it differently or come at it from a different identity.
🏃
Post-Workout Skin Reset
Target active women who experience skin irritation, congestion, or redness after workouts. Position the Trio as the calming post-exercise ritual that works with the skin's natural recovery process — not against it. The TCM "reset" language maps naturally to the athletic recovery mindset.
🧪
Skincare Skeptic Conversion
Target women who think most skincare is overhyped marketing, but who remain curious about traditional and heritage approaches. Test whether the TCM origin story — ancient remedy, not a trend — converts people who distrust the beauty industry and its claims. Lead with transparency and science, not lifestyle imagery.
🌿
Wellness Burnout Recovery
Target former wellness enthusiasts who burned out on complicated routines and the mental load of tracking supplements, protocols, and self-optimization. Test whether the simplicity of ancient TCM — a 3-step system that has existed for centuries without needing an app — appeals to people tired of modern wellness culture.
🏠
Minimalist Mom
Target busy mothers who have embraced minimalism in home organization, meal planning, or wardrobe, but still face chaos in their skincare routine. Test whether the "one trio, five minutes, every day" message resonates with people who have made simplicity a deliberate lifestyle choice elsewhere but not yet applied it to skincare.
🌸
Tatcha Alternative Discovery
Target women who love Tatcha's Eastern beauty philosophy and ingredient transparency but find the price point difficult to sustain. Lead with direct price comparison and social proof from converted Tatcha customers. The TCM ingredient story positions Moonbow as philosophically aligned but more accessible and honest about pricing.

What & Why
The current LP (get.moonbowskin.com) is a strong product page. What it does not do is warm up colder or more skeptical audiences before asking for the purchase. Two new formats are proposed for May testing, each designed to convert traffic that does not yet trust the brand enough to buy on first contact.
Advertorial LP

Reads like an editorial — converts like an ad

Written in the style of a magazine article or blog post. No obvious ads tone. Tells a narrative story ("I spent 3 years trying to fix my reactive skin") before introducing Moonbow as the solution. Ideal for skeptical or cold audiences who would bounce from a direct product page. Strong fit for the Skincare Skeptic and Wellness Burnout angles.

Listicle LP

Scannable, specific, trust-building

Structured as a ranked or numbered list ("5 reasons why reactive skin is not actually sensitive skin"). Each point educates, builds trust, and moves toward the product naturally. Ideal for audiences who engage with content but need education before purchase. Strong fit for the Post-Workout and Tatcha Alternative angles.

CRO Findings — get.moonbowskin.com
How We Approached This
We reviewed the full LP from the perspective of a first-time visitor arriving from a cold ad. The findings below are specific conversion friction points — not layout suggestions — each with a clear hypothesis for why it is costing purchases.
1
Checkout Plan Framing Creates Friction at Decision Point
High Impact
The "Choose Your Plan" section presents Subscribe & Save ($68.80) and One-Time ($86.00) side by side. Subscribe is pre-selected and marked "Best Value," which is good. But the $17.20 price difference with an auto-delivery commitment is a significant ask for a first-time buyer who has no trust in the product yet. Many people abandoning checkout are likely hesitating here — they want to try once, but the One-Time price feels steep relative to the subscription. Hypothesis: Adding a "first order discount" applied automatically to the one-time price (e.g. 10% off first purchase) — visible at the plan selection stage, not just in a popup — would reduce abandonment from undecided first-time buyers without cannibalising subscription revenue.
2
Fake Urgency Signals May Be Eroding Trust
High Impact
The LP uses three urgency tactics simultaneously: a countdown timer in the announcement bar, "Limited stock · 46 viewing" with a live timer, and "01:27:03 left." Buyers in 2026 are highly sensitive to manufactured urgency — these elements are widely recognised as tactics rather than genuine signals. For a brand whose core identity is honesty and transparency, fake scarcity messaging is a trust mismatch. Hypothesis: Replacing the fabricated countdown and "viewing now" widget with a genuine social proof signal (e.g. "11,500+ customers · ships in 2–3 days") would better align with the brand voice and potentially recover trust-sensitive buyers who bounce at this stage.
3
Money-Back Guarantee Not Prominent Enough at Purchase Decision
Medium Impact
The 30-Day Guarantee badge appears below the CTA button in small text alongside "Free Shipping" and "Made in USA." For a $86 first-time purchase of a new skincare brand, the guarantee is one of the most powerful purchase triggers available — it removes risk entirely. It is currently undersold. Hypothesis: Elevating the guarantee copy to appear above the CTA, with a one-line explanation ("Try it for 30 days. If your skin doesn't respond, we refund you. No questions."), would directly address the hesitation moment immediately before clicking buy.
4
Gua Sha — Out-of-Stock Upsell Opportunity Being Missed
Medium Impact
The Gua Sha tool is referenced on the site but currently out of stock. This is an untapped upsell and retention lever. Proposed action: Add a "Gua Sha — Coming Soon" section to the LP with a waitlist email capture. This serves two purposes: (1) it signals that the brand is expanding and active, building confidence in the brand's longevity; (2) it captures emails from buyers who are interested in accessories, creating a warm remarketing audience for when stock returns. For current buyers, it plants the upsell intent before they have even made the first purchase.
5
Price Transparency Section Is Strong — But Buried Too Deep
Medium Impact
The competitor comparison table (Moonbow vs La Mer vs Drunk Elephant, showing $1.15/day cost) is genuinely compelling and one of the best objection-handling elements on the page. It is currently positioned very far down — most mobile visitors will never see it. Hypothesis: Moving the cost-per-day framing ($1.15/day) to appear near the price selection section — even as a single line of copy — would reframe the purchase decision from "is $86 worth it?" to "is $1.15/day worth it?" This is a proven framing shift for DTC skincare conversion.

What the video is about

Repeatable formula identified: Lifestyle setting (travel, morning ritual, post-event) + skin trigger specific to that setting + Dr Felice's calm, knowledgeable delivery + single product focus = the format to brief more of.

Immediate action: Reduce this ad's budget significantly. Do not cut it entirely — 17 purchases at this spend level shows real intent that can be converted more efficiently with the right format.

Creative Process — How It Works

Brief → Production → Review → Live → Evaluate

  • Step 1 — Skyvane writes the brief independently. We develop the creative angle, hook, format, script direction, and reference examples without needing pre-approval. Heather can share recommendations or flag concerns at any point — we welcome input — but the brief is Skyvane's ownership and does not require sign-off before production begins.
  • Step 2 — Production and delivery. After production is complete, assets are shared with Heather for review and feedback. We work in bi-weekly batches of approximately 100+ assets — enough to cover a full 2-week testing period. Batch size is adjustable based on budget and testing priorities.
  • Step 3 — Heather reviews and gives consolidated feedback. We ask for one round of consolidated feedback per batch. Multiple separate feedback rounds extend the timeline and delay launch, which directly delays our ability to act on performance data.
  • Step 4 — Assets go live in the Prospecting / Scaling campaign. All new creatives launch within the existing campaign structure with controlled ad set monitoring. Each asset is given a minimum spend threshold before any performance judgment is made. We monitor at the ad set level and act immediately if we see fatigue or performance decline — no waiting for the monthly call.
